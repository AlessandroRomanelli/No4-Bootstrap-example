About us

Historical Contest

The British Commandos were formed during the Second World War in June 1940, following a request from the Prime Minister of the United Kingdom, Winston Churchill, for a force that could carry out hard hitting attacks on German positions on any front.

What we do

Using historical sources we replicate the conflict of the Second World War as closely as possible within the Arma sandbox. Fight the enemy back into their holes using the finely recreated historical gear, weapons and vehicles of the conflict. Gun down an entire enemy squad firing a Bren gun, or ambush a patrol with silenced Sten guns. Paradrop into the most iconic scenarios whilst divisions of tanks support your advance deep into enemy territory.

What makes us so special?

When somebody joins the No.4 Commando unit he's already guaranteed a unique, immersive experience. Fight the Evil-doers back into their holes using the finely recreated historical gear, weapons and vehicles of the WW2 Era. Gun down an entire enemy squad firing a Bren gun, or sneak up a patrol with silenced Sten guns, or paradrop into the most iconic scenarios whilst divisions of tanks support your advanced deep within enemy territory. The possibilities are endless. Whilst on one side our unit is supported by skilled developers and creators, it also has great mission makers, who make for an extra-degree of immersion when it comes to stage an operation.

Pre-Deployment

Between campaigns

The unit undergoes periods of intensive training to perfect and fine-tune its elements. Commandos trained in the Scottish Highlands before being deployed in any of their major campaigns.

COMPLETED

Polish Campaign

1st September — 6th October 1939

Nazi Germany invaded Poland in late '39. British Commandos were deployed in Poland to lay groundwork for the greater British Expeditionary Force to step in. The BEF never got there: the opening of a second, opposite front by the Soviets caused the Commandos evaucation and Poland's surrender.

COMPLETED

Finnish Campaign

30th November 1939 — 13th March 1940

When the Soviets invaded Finland in the late '40, the British Commandos were immediately dispatched to help the Finnish Troops. By the time a bigger Franco-British force could be deployed the war was already over: the Soviets had won but that came at a price of heavy casualties.

COMPLETED

Norwegian Campaign

9th April — 10th June 1940

British and French forces came to Norway's aid with an expeditionary force in early April. Despite the successes of the Commandos in the northern regions, German forces eventually took over the country in 62 days. Once again the German Army had proved their strength and ruthlessness.

COMPLETED

Invasion of France

10th May — 25th June 1940

Allies were all caught by surprise when the German Army unexpectedly pushed through and conquered the Netherlands, Luxemburg and Belgium and attacked the French front on their less covered side. The BEF fought bravely through the Ardennes but was forced to retreat to Dunkirk and leave the mainland.

COMPLETED

Libyan Desert Campaign

10th June 1940 — 13th May 1943

The Italians sided with Germany and declared war on the Allies, a few days later the British Army was already crossing the border from Egypt into Lybia, where Italy had its colonial interests. It was the start of a series of brutal fights, before the formidable Deutsches Afrika Korps stepped in, led by Erwin Rommel, known as "The Desert Fox".

ONGOING

Italian Campaign

10th July 1943 — 2nd May 1945

Starting with the invasion of Sicily, the Italian campaign was carried out by the Allied coalition and led to the arrest of Benito Mussolini, known as "Il Duce" or "Dux" and Italy's surrender. The Axis lost a valuable ally in the war but it still wasn't giving up on the buffer ground that Italy represented, hence the German occupation of Italy began.

PLANNED

Western Europe Campaign

6 June 1944 — 8 May 1945

The second part of the Western Campaign takes place years after the capitulation of France and sees the Allies making their comeback. Starting off from the Normandy's landings, back through France, Belgium, the Netherlands and all the way to Berlin where the German Army prepared its last stand.

TO BE ANNOUNCED

Eastern Europe Campaign

22nd June 1941 — 9th May 1945

The Eastern Front was active throughout most of the war. The UK took initially part to the fight only in the form of Lend-Lease programmes but later decided to secretly send the Commandos to turn the tides in the Soviets' favor.

Join the unit

Requirements:

  1. Age: you must be at least 16 years old, anyone below that requires special acceptance into the unit;
  2. Attendance: should be able to attend at least two operations per week in a normal situation;
  3. Exclusivity: applicants must not be part of any other ArmA Units upon joining;

Contacts:

  • Cpt. C. Foote

  • SMaj. A. Hyde

  • SSgt. A. Roman

How to enlist:

  1. Contact one of the unit leaders by adding them on Steam or joining the Teamspeak server;

  2. A leader will carry out an interview with the applicant;

  3. Enter the unit:
    • Accept the invite the to No.4 Steam Group;
    • Change your name to a valid format (e.g. W. White);
    • Append the [No.4] tag to your steam name;
    • Download the mod collection and ask for access to our private mod;

  4. Attend a Basic Combat Training (BCT);

  5. Attend an unit operation.

After going through all the steps listed above you'll be promoted and officially part of the Commandos: at this time you will be asked which position you'd like to have.

If you would like to join our unit or make any enquiries into what we do then feel free to add one of our friendly leaders using Steam or join our Teamspeak at any time.

Helping the unit


Interested in making the unit grow?

Any of these steps can help us out in delivering a better overall experience:


  • ——  Recruit people

    Word of mouth is the best weapon in this case! Get on a public server, spread the word about the commandos and get your friends involved.


  • ——  Give your input

    Become a developer, mission builder, moderator or leader! Share your skills with us and just be creative: every little helps.


  • ——  Donate

    Recent studies have shown that servers do not run for free, therefore any donation that you can make is more than welcome.

Having a big, wealthy community not only helps us afford the maintenance of the server, but also allows us to expand the unit with additional, specialised sections like Flight, Armor and Weapons.
